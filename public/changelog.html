What's New in Meter AI

Meter AI evolves continuously based on community feedback, browser updates, and AI workspace enhancements.

Why We Publish Updates

Meter AI evolves continuously as AI platforms change and new workflows emerge. This page highlights meaningful improvements that change how the product feels to use, rather than documenting every internal engineering task.

Product Evolution & Enhancements

Tracking & Insights

We have improved the local calculations to help you stay ahead of rate limits without losing focus on your prompt loops.

  • Live capacity updates: Live visual indicators help you understand how much of your available conversation capacity has been used.
  • Conservative estimating: Estimates are calculated more conservatively to prevent sudden limits lockouts.
  • Active session timers: Estimates for remaining counts and timer sync schedules refresh when you switch threads.

Context Bridge

Seamlessly transition active workspaces when rate limits trigger.

  • Preserved formatting: Transfer active conversations to another supported AI model with formatting preserved as much as possible.
  • Single-click migration: Quickly extract the conversation structure and copy it with clean formatting to avoid code block drift.

Everyday Improvements

Enhancements designed to make daily session management more intuitive.

  • Responsive settings toolbar: Manage your notification choices and warning thresholds directly from the toolbar extension menu.
  • Cross-browser layouts: Visual styling is optimized for Google Chrome, Brave, Arc, and Microsoft Edge browser window formats.

What We're Exploring

We are researching additional ways to improve your daily workflows. Here is a preview of updates currently in development:

  • Support for additional AI platforms.
  • Smarter usage insights and visual history dashboards.
  • Better workflow automation features.
  • Accessibility and experience enhancements.

If you updated Meter AI today, your session limits will feel more predictable, your context migrations will be smoother, and your settings panel will operate more quietly.
